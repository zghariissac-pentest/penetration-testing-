# What Is Active Directory?

Active Directory (AD) is Microsoft’s centralized directory service used to manage users, computers, groups, permissions, and security policies inside a Windows-based network. It acts as the organization’s identity and access control system, making sure the right people can access the right resources with the right level of permission.

In simple terms, AD is the central brain of an enterprise environment. Every login, permission check, password rule, group membership, and policy enforcement is handled through AD. Instead of configuring each device manually, everything is controlled from one place , the Domain Controller.

## Organizations rely on Active Directory to:

Authenticate users and systems
Control access to files, apps, servers, and services
Apply security policies across the network
Organize resources into a structured, manageable hierarchy

Because AD manages almost everything related to identity and permissions, it becomes one of the most important , and most targeted , components in corporate networks. Understanding what AD is sets the foundation for understanding how modern Windows environments truly operate.
